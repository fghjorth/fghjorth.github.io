About me

I'm a Ph.D. Candidate at the Department of Political Science, University of Copenhagen.

My educational background is in political science, and before starting working towards the Ph.D., I worked for a few fun, intense years at Denmark's Ministry of Finance. My background is outlined in more detail in my curriculum vitae.

Beyond my Ph.D. project I'm passionate about American politics, doing data science with R, and using experimental methods to make people and organizations smarter. Privately I live in Nørrebro, Copenhagen, with my lovely fiancée Ida.

What my dissertation is about

Focusing on European welfare states, I study how rhetoric and context can shape political cognition and behavior by invoking group identities. In a diversifying Europe, group identities are likely to become ever more salient. I hope to add to our understanding of when and how this matters for politics. You can read more in my project description, The dog that didn't bark: Group implication and welfare state politics
