What my dissertation is about


Above is one of my favorite plots in political science. It shows American respondents' support for imprisonment as a tool for fighting crime, depending on how they feel about blacks. As the plot shows, referring to crime as "inner city" crime dramatically increases punitive attitudes among those most negative toward blacks. The phrase "inner city" seems to have somehow made the issue of crime prevention a racial one.

This is in many ways what my project is about. Focusing on European welfare states, I try to look at how rhetoric can shape political cognition and behavior by appealing to group identities. In a diversifying Europe, group identities are likely to become ever more salient. I hope to add to our understanding of when and how this matters for politics. You can read more in my project description, The dog that didn't bark: Group implication and welfare state politics
