About me

I'm a Ph.D. Candidate at the Department of Political Science, University of Copenhagen. I will defend my Ph.D. dissertation in May 2016, after which I will be a post.doc. at the Department of Political Science.

My educational background is in political science, and before going to grad school, I worked for a few fun, intense years at Denmark's Ministry of Finance. My background is outlined in more detail in my curriculum vitae.

Beyond my Ph.D. project I'm passionate about American politics, doing data science with R, good food, and football. Privately I live in Nørrebro, Copenhagen, with my lovely wife, Ida.

My Ph.D. dissertation

Focusing on European welfare states, I study how rhetoric and context can shape political cognition and behavior by invoking group identities. In a diversifying Europe, group identities are likely to become ever more salient. I hope to add to our understanding of when and how this matters for politics. My dissertation, which I will defend on May 12th, 2016, is called Ethnicization in Welfare State Politics.

My book "Poligak"

Asmus Olsen and myself have written a book, "Poligak - Hvordan politikerne prøver at snøre dig - og hvorfor du næsten altid snyder dig selv". It's sort of a Danish-language Freakonomics based on political science research.

Poligak came out October 21st, 2013. You can read more about it at poligak.dk
